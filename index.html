HOW THE MAGIC HAPPENED

The extraordinary story of how soft-bodied creatures became perfectly preserved fossils

1

Ancient Tropical Reef

508 million years ago, this region lay near the equator under warm tropical seas. A massive underwater limestone cliff (the Cathedral Escarpment) towered 160 meters high, built by sponges and algae.

LOCATION: Ancient North America (Laurentia) ‚Ä¢ DEPTH: Shallow tropical sea
2

Thriving Deep-Water Community

At the base of this cliff, in a deeper basin, fine mud accumulated and supported a rich marine ecosystem. Anomalocaris, Hallucigenia, and hundreds of other strange creatures lived on this soft muddy seafloor.

HABITAT: Deep muddy basin ‚Ä¢ COMMUNITY: 170+ species discovered
3

Catastrophic Burial

Periodic underwater mudslides, triggered by earthquakes or storms, swept down from the reef. These mudflows instantly smothered the seafloor community in fine sediment, burying animals alive before they could decay.

TRIGGER: Earthquakes/storms ‚Ä¢ RESULT: Instant burial in oxygen-poor mud
4

Exceptional Preservation

The oxygen-poor mud prevented decay and scavenging. Soft tissues like eyes, gills, and digestive tracts were preserved as thin carbon films. Over millions of years, the mud compressed into shale rock, creating our "time capsule."

PRESERVATION: Soft tissues as carbon films ‚Ä¢ DURATION: 508 million years
üî¨

Why This Matters

Normally, soft tissues decay within days. The Burgess Shale's unique conditions created "Burgess Shale-type preservation" ‚Äì capturing delicate anatomy that almost never fossilizes. We can see muscle fibers, digestive tracts, and even the contents of ancient stomachs!

THE DISCOVERY

A tale of scientific revolution that changed our understanding of life itself

üèîÔ∏è
1909

The Accident That Changed History

Tap to learn more

Charles Doolittle Walcott, a renowned Smithsonian paleontologist, was exploring these mountains on horseback when he noticed a slab of shale rich with strange fossils. Intrigued, he returned the next year with his wife and children to establish a quarry on Fossil Ridge.

Year after year until 1924, Walcott led packhorses up to the quarry and split the shale layers. Working within the scientific views of his time, he initially tried to fit these bizarre creatures into known groups, and for decades many were dismissed as oddities or "failed" species.

Over 65,000 specimens collected ‚Ä¢ Shipped to Washington D.C. ‚Ä¢ Founded the science of exceptional preservation

"He had discovered an ancient ecosystem, frozen in stone."
üî¨
1966-1971

The Scientific Revolution

Tap to learn more

A new generation of researchers, led by Harry B. Whittington of Cambridge, reopened the Burgess Shale story. With fresh eyes and better microscopes, Whittington and his students Simon Conway Morris and Derek Briggs painstakingly re-examined Walcott's fossils.

They realized many were unlike any living animals. Opabinia (with five eyes and a hose-like snout) and Hallucigenia (so odd it was originally reconstructed upside-down!) didn't fit into traditional groups.

The "Burgess Revision" revealed a much richer and stranger Cambrian world than anyone had imagined

"Fundamentally changed our understanding of early animal evolution"

BIZARRE LIFE

Meet the strangest creatures that ever lived

Anomalocaris

canadensis

The apex predator of the Cambrian seas. This formidable hunter was a torpedo-shaped animal with large compound eyes on stalks and a pair of spiny, segmented appendages at the front.

These appendages were like deadly tongs, grabbing prey such as trilobites and feeding them into a round, pineapple-slice-like mouth made of hardened plates with serrated edges. Its eyes were some of the most advanced ever found in 500-million-year-old rocks.

Opabinia

regalis

Imagine a soft, segmented creature with five eyes perched on its head and a long flexible proboscis like an elephant's trunk ‚Äì except this "trunk" ends in a claw!

Its mouth faced backwards, and it used that claw-tipped proboscis to probe mud and snag worms. When first revealed to scientists in the 1970s, legend has it the audience burst into laughter.

LENGTH: 7CM
Size of a computer mouse

Hallucigenia

sparsa

A worm-like animal with seven pairs of rigid spines along its back and seven pairs of soft, tentacle-like legs on its underside.

For decades scientists drew it upside-down and backwards! They finally found its true head ‚Äì with simple eyes and a tooth-lined throat ‚Äì in 2015. An early ancestor of today's velvet worms.

LENGTH: 0.5-5CM
Size of a paperclip to thumb

Marrella

splendens

Delicate arthropod known as the "lace crab." The most common Burgess Shale fossil, with spidery legs and delicate antennae.

An early relative of today's spiders and scorpions, Marrella helped scientists understand how arthropods diversified in ancient marine ecosystems.

LENGTH: 2CM
Size of a quarter coin

Wiwaxia

corrugata

A "slug in chainmail" - soft underneath but covered in overlapping armored scales and two rows of long spines like a spiky mohawk.

This heavily armored herbivore scraped algae from the seafloor with two grinding plates. Its defensive spines likely protected it from the newly evolving predators.

LENGTH: 5CM
Size of a large thumb

Pikaia

gracilens

Our ancient ancestor! This small, eel-like creature is one of the earliest known chordates - the group that includes all vertebrates.

With segmented muscle bands and a primitive backbone, Pikaia represents the humble beginnings of the lineage that would eventually lead to fish, mammals, and ultimately us.

LENGTH: 5CM
Size of a large thumb

  • ‚Ä¢ Drove evolution of better eyes and nervous systems

  • ‚Ä¢ Led to burrowing and hiding behaviors

ü§ù Reconciling Evolution with Latter-day Saint Faith

Faith vs. Science or Faith and Science?

Can a faithful member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints accept the science of evolution? Many Latter-day Saint scholars and leaders have found ways to harmonize their faith with scientific findings. Rather than seeing science and scripture as bitter enemies, we can view them as answering different questions.

The Problem of "Concordism"

Concordism assumes that science and scripture must agree perfectly in their details about creation. If there's a mismatch, you must choose one or the other. However, this assumption is flawed - the Bible was never intended to give a modern scientific account of how God created the world.

Interpreting Genesis in Context

The Bible reflects an ancient worldview. Scripture often speaks in terms of the worldview of its original audience. God taught spiritual truths using their existing understanding. Genesis was given to teach theological lessons about God's power and purpose, not to reveal scientific details of Earth's natural history.

Key Point:

"If I have a non-scientific creation account, but I try to read it as scientific history, that's not a literal reading. That's a misreading." - Ben Spackman

LDS History: Early Debates and Evolving Views

Two scientist-apostles, Elder James E. Talmage and Elder John A. Widtsoe, were quite open to evolution and saw scientific discoveries as reinforcing their faith. They viewed natural laws as evidence of God's work.

President David O. McKay (1951-1970) was personally friendly toward evolutionary theory, once stating that "Genesis in no way prohibits evolution" in the origins of human bodies. He approved scientific articles about DNA and evolution for Church magazines.

Current Church Position

The Church has explicitly stated that "the Church has no official position on the theory of evolution", calling it "a matter for scientific study." What the Church does affirm is why we are here and who we are: children of God, created in His image.

Official Statement:

"The scriptures tell why man was created, but they do not tell how."

Science and Religion: Different Questions

Science deals with the natural world‚Äîprocesses, evidence, and mechanisms‚Äîwhile religion deals with spiritual truths‚Äîmeaning, morality, and purpose. They answer different questions:

üî¨ Science Asks:

  • ‚Ä¢ How do natural processes work?
  • ‚Ä¢ What evidence do we observe?
  • ‚Ä¢ What mechanisms explain phenomena?

‚ú® Religion Asks:

  • ‚Ä¢ Why are we here?
  • ‚Ä¢ What is our purpose?
  • ‚Ä¢ What does it mean to be human?

Finding Harmony

President Russell M. Nelson taught that "there is no conflict between science and religion. Conflict only arises from an incomplete knowledge of either science or religion (or both)."

Many faithful LDS scientists find that evolution is one of the natural processes instituted by God. At BYU, evolution is taught robustly, and faculty have created resources to help students reconcile these truths.

üí´ "All Truth Is Part of the Gospel"

Latter-day Saints believe God is the Master of the universe. Whether He chose to form our bodies over eons through evolution or in an instant is ultimately secondary to the truth that we are His children with a divine destiny.

"No evidence denies the hand of God in directing evolution." - Duane Jeffery, BYU Professor
