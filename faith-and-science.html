Faith & Science: LDS Perspectives on Evolution

A comprehensive exploration of how faith and science can harmoniously coexist

Faith and Science or Faith vs. Science?

Members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints have long grappled with questions about evolution, the age of the Earth, and scriptural creation accounts. Do modern scientific discoveries irreconcilably conflict with the Bible and LDS doctrine? Some assume that belief in biblical creation automatically conflicts with modern evolutionary biology. However, many Latter-day Saint scholars and leaders have found ways to harmonize their faith with scientific findings.

Key Insight from Ben Spackman:

"Can evolution be reconciled with the Bible?" - Yes, "but not the way people think." Rather than seeing science and scripture as bitter enemies, we can view them as answering different questions.

‚ñº Part 1: The Problem of "Concordism"

Overview:

One key issue underlying faith-science conflicts is "concordism" ‚Äì the assumption that science and scripture must speak the exact same language and agree in every detail, or one of them must be false.

The Concordist Assumption

Concordism is, as Spackman explains, "an assumption that science, especially evolution or the age of the earth ‚Ä¶ and scripture are speaking the same language. They're talking about the same thing and therefore they have to match up or one of them is false." This mindset leads to two common extremes:

Young-Earth Creationists

Twist science to fit a literal reading of the Bible (claiming Earth is only thousands of years old)

Forced Metaphorizers

Reinterpret Genesis metaphorically to force a fit with current science

Think of it this way:

When the Psalmist says "the sun rises" (Psalm 104:22), we don't reject astronomy because we know the Earth rotates. Similarly, when Moses describes photosynthesis occurring before the sun appears (Genesis 1), it's perfectly fine - he's teaching theology, not botany.

Why Concordism is Flawed

Spackman argues that this underlying assumption is flawed. The belief that Genesis is providing a natural history of the earth, a physical history of the earth as a science textbook would is "simply not an accurate assumption." The Bible was never intended to give a modern scientific account of how God created the world. When we drop the expectation that scripture and science speak in the same terms, the supposed conflict between faith and evolution looks very different.

‚ñº Part 2: Interpreting Genesis in Context

Overview:

The Bible reflects an ancient worldview that God used to teach spiritual truths to ancient people. Understanding this context is crucial for proper interpretation.

The Ancient Worldview

The Bible reflects an ancient worldview‚Äîfor example, its writers assumed a flat, motionless earth beneath a solid dome-like sky. Today we know the earth is a sphere orbiting the sun‚Äîan idea once deemed "flatly contrary to scripture"! Yet accepting a round Earth poses no crisis of faith for believers.

Key Point:

Scripture often speaks in the terms of the worldview of its original audience. God did not reveal astronomy to ancient Israel; instead, He taught spiritual truths using their existing cosmic understanding.

What "Literal" Actually Means

Historically, to read scripture "literally" meant to read it "according to the author's intent," with full context of genre and background. A truly literal reading recognizes when a biblical passage is poetic or symbolic.

Augustine's Definition (AD 400):

"A literal reading requires expertise‚Äîreading poetry as poetry, fiction as fiction, history as history. It doesn't mean a surface reading without context or a scientific reading."

Overview:

Early 20th-century LDS leaders had diverse views on evolution, from enthusiastic acceptance to strong opposition. Understanding this history shows that LDS doctrine was never monolithic on evolution.

1930s Church Response

President Heber J. Grant (1931): The Brethren should "leave Geology, Biology, Archaeology and Anthropology ‚Ä¶ to scientific research" rather than fight over these subjects. The Church chose neutrality.

President David O. McKay's Open Approach

President McKay (1951-1970) was personally friendly toward scientific inquiry and evolutionary theory. He once said "Genesis in no way prohibits evolution" and approved pro-evolution articles for Church publications.

Key Example:

A BYU professor submitted an article on DNA and evolution to a Church magazine. McKay read it and said "This is fantastic work. Run it," but included a disclaimer that it wasn't official Church doctrine.

‚ñº Part 4: Specific Questions & Clarifications

Did Humans Evolve from Apes?

‚ùå Misconception: "Evolution claims we came from monkeys"

‚úÖ Reality: Humans and apes share common ancestors

"You think we came from apes? Well, Genesis says we came from dust. Is that really so much better?" - Ben Spackman

Does the Bible Support a Flat Earth?

Historical Reality: Biblical Hebrews assumed a flat earth. Some verses imply geocentric, flat-earth cosmology.

Modern LDS Position: We accept a globe orbiting the sun, despite this being "flatly contrary to scripture" if taken literally.

The Lesson:

This shows us how to handle evolution‚Äîunderstand ancient context, don't expect technical scientific knowledge in scripture.

BYU and Evolution Education

At Church-owned schools, evolution is taught robustly. The BYU Biology Department's "Reconciling Evolution" initiative helps students see that one can accept evolution and remain a committed believer.

President Russell M. Nelson:

"There is no conflict between science and religion. Conflict only arises from an incomplete knowledge of either science or religion (or both)."

Conclusion: "All Truth Is Part of the Gospel"

üåü Faith + Science in Action

Latter-day Saints believe God is the Master of the universe, the architect of life. Whether He chose to form our bodies over eons through evolution or in an instant is ultimately secondary to the truth that we are His children with a divine destiny.

The Gospel and evolution answer different aspects of truth. The Gospel teaches who we are and why we are here. Evolutionary science can enlighten us as to how God's creative processes might have unfolded over time.

"No evidence denies the hand of God in directing evolution." - Duane Jeffery, BYU Professor

A Balanced Approach

An LDS member can confidently accept evolution where strong evidence exists while still having faith in divine purpose and miracles. This requires:

1. Careful Scripture Interpretation

Let it teach the "why" rather than forcing scientific "how" answers

2. Scientific Humility

Acknowledge what science can and cannot determine

3. Faith in Truth

Trust that all truth comes from God
