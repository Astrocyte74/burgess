Faith and Science or Faith vs. Science?

Members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS) have long grappled with questions about evolution, the age of the Earth, and scriptural creation accounts. Key Insight from Ben Spackman:

"Can evolution be reconciled with the Bible?" - Yes, "but not the way people think." Rather than seeing science and scripture as bitter enemies, we can view them as answering different questions.

Overview:

One key issue underlying faith-science conflicts is "concordism" ‚Äì the assumption that science and scripture must speak the exact same language and agree in every detail, or one of them must be false.

The Concordist Assumption

Concordism The assumption that scriptural texts and scientific accounts must align detail-for-detail; otherwise one must be false. is, as Spackman explains, "an assumption that science, especially evolution or the age of the earth ‚Ä¶ and scripture are speaking the same language. They're talking about the same thing and therefore they have to match up or one of them is false." This mindset leads to two common extremes:

Young-Earth Creationists

Twist science to fit a literal reading of the Bible (claiming Earth is only thousands of years old)

Forced Metaphorizers

Reinterpret Genesis metaphorically to force a fit with current science

Think of it this way:

When the Psalmist says "the sun rises" (Psalm 104:22), we don't reject astronomy because we know the Earth rotates. Similarly, when Moses describes photosynthesis occurring before the sun appears (Genesis 1), it's perfectly fine - he's teaching theology, not botany.

Why Concordism is Flawed

Spackman argues that this underlying assumption is flawed. The belief that Genesis is providing a natural history of the earth, a physical history of the earth as a science textbook would is "simply not an accurate assumption." The Bible was never intended to give a modern scientific account of how God created the world. When we drop the expectation that scripture and science speak in the same terms, the supposed conflict between faith and evolution looks very different.

An LDS member can confidently accept evolution where strong evidence exists while still having faith in divine purpose and miracles. This requires:
